# Jamal Reeves: The Second-Round Steal

## Montverde to Gonzaga

Jamal Reeves was supposed to be a project. Selected 38th overall in the 2021 NBA Draft -- the kind of pick teams use on draft-and-stash Europeans or long-shot gambles -- Reeves has spent four seasons systematically exceeding every expectation placed upon him.

Born November 8, 2001, in Orlando, Florida, Reeves honed his skills at Montverde Academy, one of the premier high school basketball programs in the country. At 6'7" with a 6'11" wingspan, he had the physical tools of a lottery pick but the shooting numbers of a late second-rounder. His senior year -- 16.8 points, 6.4 rebounds, 1.8 steals -- was impressive but not eye-popping enough for the blue-blood programs. Gonzaga took a chance.

## The Bulldog Development

At Gonzaga, Reeves transformed. Under Mark Few's system, he learned to leverage his length defensively while developing a reliable mid-range game. His sophomore season (2020-21) was the breakthrough: 15.2 points, 6.1 rebounds, and WCC All-Conference First Team honors. His defensive metrics were elite -- opponents shot 38% when Reeves was the primary defender, a number that caught the attention of NBA scouts.

## Charlotte's Hidden Gem

The Hornets selected Reeves with the 38th pick, viewing him as a long-term defensive project with scoring upside. His rookie year (2021-22) was modest: 8.4 points and 4.2 rebounds in 18 minutes off the bench. But the defensive impact was immediate -- Charlotte's defensive rating dropped by 4.2 points per 100 possessions with Reeves on the floor.

Each subsequent season has brought measurable improvement. His scoring climbed from 8.4 to 11.6 to 14.2 to 15.8 points per game, a trajectory of steady, sustainable growth that front offices dream about. His three-point shooting, once a glaring weakness (31.2% as a rookie), has improved to a respectable 35.6% in 2024-25.

## The Defensive Anchor

Reeves' calling card remains his defense. His combination of length (6'11" wingspan), lateral quickness, and anticipation makes him one of the premier perimeter defenders in the league. In 2024-25, he averaged 1.5 steals and 0.9 blocks per game -- rare versatility for a wing -- earning his first All-Defensive Second Team selection.

His Clamps badge (Gold tier) is well-earned. Reeves is the type of defender who can switch 1-through-4, chase guards around screens, and contest shots at the rim. The Interceptor badge (also Gold) reflects his knack for reading passing lanes; his 1.5 steals per game rank among the top wings in the NBA.

## The Contract and the Future

In July 2024, Charlotte signed Reeves to a three-year, $45 million extension -- a bargain for a player of his caliber. The MCL sprain that cost him 12 games in February 2024 was the only significant injury of his career, and he returned without missing a beat.

At 23, Reeves is a cornerstone of Charlotte's defensive identity. Alongside Marcus Thompson's playmaking and Sebastian Wilkins' rim protection, Reeves forms the third pillar of a young core that has the city buzzing about playoff basketball for the first time in years.

## Playing Style

Reeves is a slasher first, shooter second. His 72% drive tendency reflects a player who attacks the basket with conviction, using his length to finish over and around defenders. The Slithery Finisher badge (Silver) captures his ability to contort his body through contact. His post-up tendency is low (12%), but when he does operate in the mid-post, his face-up game is effective.

The three-point shot remains a work in progress. At 35.6%, it's functional enough to keep defenders honest, but not yet a primary weapon. If Reeves can push that number to 38-39%, he'll unlock another tier of his offensive game.
