# Cameron Sheffield: The Sharpshooter's Journey

## A Shooter is Born

Cameron Sheffield grew up in Memphis, Tennessee, spending summers at the Lester Community Center gym where his uncle, a former college player at Memphis State, taught him the art of shooting. By age 12, Sheffield could knock down three-pointers with a consistency that unnerved opposing coaches. At Bartlett High School, he was a two-year starter who averaged 18.2 points as a senior, shooting 40.2% from three on high volume.

The SEC came calling. Sheffield chose Tennessee, drawn by the program's commitment to player development and Rick Barnes' track record of producing NBA guards.

## Volunteer Years

Sheffield's freshman season at Tennessee (2020-21) was a learning curve. He shot well (38.8% from three) but struggled to create his own shot, averaging 10.8 points in a reserve role. The breakthrough came as a sophomore: 15.4 points per game, 41.2% from three, and SEC All-Conference Second Team honors. His catch-and-shoot ability was elite -- 47.8% on spot-up threes according to Synergy data -- and NBA scouts took notice.

Sheffield declared for the 2022 draft after his sophomore season, confident his shooting would translate.

## Lakers and the Trade

The Los Angeles Lakers selected Sheffield 22nd overall in the 2022 Draft, hoping his shooting would complement their star-driven offense. His rookie season showed promise: 9.2 points and 37.8% from three in 20 minutes per game, earning All-Rookie Second Team honors.

But the fit was never quite right. The Lakers' half-court offense demanded more self-creation than Sheffield could provide, and his role fluctuated between starter and bench player. Midway through his second season, with Sheffield averaging 11.4 points and shooting 39.2% from deep, the Lakers made their move.

On February 8, 2024, Sheffield was traded to the Charlotte Hornets in a multi-player deal. It was the kind of trade that barely registers on the national radar but transforms a franchise.

## Finding Home in Charlotte

The trade to Charlotte was a revelation. Alongside Marcus Thompson -- whose elite playmaking generated open looks that Sheffield's Laker teammates never could -- Sheffield's shooting exploded. In 26 games with the Hornets to close the 2023-24 season, he averaged 12.8 points and shot 40.5% from three. His Catch & Shoot badge (Gold) and Corner Specialist badge (Gold) were on full display.

The 2024-25 season has been Sheffield's best. Starting all 58 games alongside Thompson, Reeves, and Wilkins, he's averaging 13.4 points and shooting 41.2% from three. His three-point volume has increased (6.8 attempts per game) without sacrificing efficiency, a sign of growing confidence and better shot selection.

## Defensive Growth

Sheffield entered the league as a defensive liability, but his work ethic has transformed that weakness into a strength. His Clamps badge (Silver) reflects genuine improvement in on-ball defense, and his 1.2 steals per game suggest active hands and good anticipation. The combination of shooting and defense makes him one of the more valuable role players in the Eastern Conference.

## What's Ahead

Sheffield's rookie-scale contract expires after the 2025-26 season, and Charlotte faces a decision. As a restricted free agent, they'll have the right to match any offer, and Sheffield's shooting makes him a coveted commodity on the open market. At 22 years old, he's still improving, and his fit alongside Charlotte's young core is seamless.
